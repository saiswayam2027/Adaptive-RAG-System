BUSINESS TRAVEL POLICY — FINANCE HANDBOOK, SECTION 7

7.1 Booking Requirements
All business travel must be booked through the corporate travel portal to
be reimbursable, except in regions where the portal has no coverage, in
which case direct booking is allowed with pre-trip approval from the
manager. This is a separate approval from the airfare-advance-booking rule
in the Expense Policy's Section 3.2, which governs timing, not the booking
channel.

7.2 International Travel Safety
Travel to a country on the elevated-risk list requires Security team
sign-off at least 5 business days before departure, and travelers must
enroll in the company's travel tracking app for the duration of the trip.
This is unrelated to the Security team approvals required for payments or
authentication service changes in Engineering.

7.3 Per Diem Rates
Meal per diems vary by destination city cost-of-living tier: Tier 1 cities
(highest cost) receive $85/day, Tier 2 receive $65/day, Tier 3 receive
$45/day. Per diems are not reduced for partial travel days except the
first and last day of a trip, which are paid at 75%.

7.4 Combining Business and Personal Travel
Employees may extend a business trip for personal reasons at their own
expense; the company covers only the cost of the business-required
portion, calculated by comparing the actual airfare to what a
business-only itinerary would have cost, documented by the travel desk.

7.5 Travel Expense Submission Deadline
Travel expenses must be submitted within 30 days of trip completion, a
shorter window than the general 60-day expense submission deadline in
Section 3.3 of the Expense Policy, because travel expenses are higher-volume
and harder to reconstruct accurately if delayed.
