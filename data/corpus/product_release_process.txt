PRODUCT RELEASE AND LAUNCH PROCESS — PRODUCT HANDBOOK, SECTION 1

1.1 Launch Readiness Review
Any customer-facing feature launch requires a Launch Readiness Review at
least 5 business days before the target date, covering support
documentation, a rollback plan, and confirmation that the change has
already passed through Engineering's canary release process described in
the Deployment Policy.

1.2 Beta and Gradual Rollout
Features enter a private beta with a named customer list before general
availability. Beta feedback issues rated as blocking must be resolved
before proceeding to the percentage-based gradual rollout, which follows
the same 1% / 10% / 100% staging pattern as Engineering's canary releases
but is tracked separately in the product analytics dashboard, not the
deployment system.

1.3 Launch Communication Ownership
Marketing owns external announcement content and timing (see Marketing
embargo rules), Product owns the changelog and in-app release notes, and
Support owns internal enablement material for the support team. All three
must be finalized before the Launch Readiness Review can pass.

1.4 Post-Launch Monitoring
For the first 72 hours after general availability, the feature owner must
monitor adoption and error metrics daily and is on point to pull the
feature via feature flag if usage-pattern issues emerge, independent of the
Sev-1/Sev-2 incident process, which only triggers for the broader classes
of outage or breach described in Security's Incident Response policy.

1.5 Launch Postponement
A Launch Readiness Review can be postponed by any of Engineering, Support,
or Legal raising an unresolved blocking concern; Marketing alone cannot
block a launch but can request a postponement through the Product lead.
