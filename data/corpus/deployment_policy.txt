DEPLOYMENT POLICY — ENGINEERING HANDBOOK, SECTION 4

4.1 Deployment Windows
All production deployments must occur between 10:00 and 16:00 IST on weekdays.
Deployments outside this window require sign-off from the on-call Engineering Manager
and must be logged in the #deploy-exceptions channel within 15 minutes of the deploy.

4.2 Approval Requirements
Any change touching the payments service requires two approvals: one from a senior
engineer on the Payments team and one from the Security team. Changes to the
authentication service require approval from the Security team lead specifically,
not just any Security team member.

4.3 Rollback Procedure
If a deployment causes error rates to exceed 2% for more than 5 minutes, the
deploying engineer must trigger an automatic rollback using the `deploy rollback`
CLI command. Manual rollbacks (via direct database edits) are prohibited except
during a declared Severity-1 incident, and even then require a second engineer to
review the rollback plan before execution.

4.4 Canary Releases
All changes to services with more than 10,000 requests per minute must go through
a canary release: 1% traffic for 10 minutes, then 10% for 20 minutes, then 100%.
Canary metrics are automatically evaluated against the error-rate and latency
thresholds defined in Section 6.2 before promotion to the next stage.

4.5 Freeze Periods
There is a deployment freeze during the last week of each fiscal quarter (the last
7 calendar days of March, June, September, and December) except for Severity-1
security patches, which may be deployed at any time with Security team approval.
