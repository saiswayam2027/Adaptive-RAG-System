PERFORMANCE REVIEW AND PROMOTION POLICY — HR HANDBOOK, SECTION 3

3.1 Review Cycle
Formal performance reviews occur twice a year, in June and December.
Employees who joined less than 90 days before a review cycle are excluded
from that cycle and reviewed in the following one instead, mirroring the
90-day threshold used for production access eligibility in Engineering
onboarding but for an unrelated reason.

3.2 Rating Scale and Calibration
Ratings range from 1 (significantly below expectations) to 5 (role model
performance). Ratings of 4 or 5 require calibration discussion across
managers within the same department before being finalized, to reduce
inconsistency in how "exceeds expectations" is applied.

3.3 Promotion Timing
Promotions are processed twice a year, aligned with the review cycle.
Off-cycle promotions require VP approval and are limited to cases of
significant scope change or retention risk, documented in a promotion
justification memo similar in spirit to the sole-source justification memo
used in Vendor Procurement, though the two processes are otherwise
unrelated.

3.4 Performance Improvement Plans (PIP)
A PIP lasts 30, 60, or 90 days depending on severity, set by the manager
with HR Business Partner sign-off. Employees on a PIP remain eligible for
their standard leave accrual and remote work arrangements as described in
the Leave and Remote Work Policy; a PIP does not itself restrict those
benefits.

3.5 Manager Feedback Requirement
Managers must document at least one written feedback note per direct report
per quarter outside the formal review cycle, stored in the HR system for
continuity if the manager changes roles.
