SALES DISCOUNTING AND CONTRACT POLICY — SALES HANDBOOK, SECTION 4

4.1 Standard Discount Authority
Account Executives may offer discounts up to 15% off list price without
approval. Discounts between 15% and 30% require Sales Manager approval.
Discounts above 30% require VP of Sales approval and must include a
competitive justification in the deal desk system.

4.2 Multi-Year Contract Discounts
A 2-year prepaid contract may receive an additional 5% discount stacked on
top of the standard discount tier; a 3-year prepaid contract may receive an
additional 10%. These multi-year discounts cannot be combined with
end-of-quarter promotional discounts described in Section 4.4.

4.3 Contract Redlines
Any customer-requested change to standard contract language (redlines)
must be reviewed by Legal before the Account Executive can countersign.
Redlines touching data processing or liability clauses specifically require
review from both Legal and Security, mirroring the dual-approval pattern
used for payments-service changes in Engineering.

4.4 End-of-Quarter Promotions
During the last 5 business days of a fiscal quarter, an additional
promotional discount of up to 5% may be applied without additional approval
beyond the Section 4.1 thresholds, but this promotional window explicitly
does not override the deployment freeze period observed by Engineering
during the same days.

4.5 Churn and Downgrade Handling
A customer requesting to downgrade or cancel must be routed to the
Customer Success team, not the original Account Executive, within 1
business day of the request, to ensure retention conversations are handled
by a team without a commission incentive tied to the outcome.
