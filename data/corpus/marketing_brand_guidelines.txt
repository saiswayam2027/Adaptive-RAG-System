MARKETING AND BRAND GUIDELINES — MARKETING HANDBOOK, SECTION 2

2.1 Approval for External Content
Any blog post, press release, or paid ad campaign above $10,000 in spend
requires Marketing Director approval. Content mentioning specific customer
names or logos requires separate written approval from that customer's
account team, tracked in the customer reference registry.

2.2 Brand Voice and Messaging
Public-facing messaging must avoid making specific uptime or security
guarantees (e.g. "100% secure") without Legal review, since such claims can
create contractual exposure beyond what the actual SLA commits to in the
Customer Support SLA Policy.

2.3 Social Media Response Escalation
Customer complaints on public social media are triaged by the social team
within 1 hour during business hours. Complaints alleging a data breach or
security issue are escalated immediately to Security and Legal
simultaneously, bypassing the normal social-team response queue entirely.

2.4 Co-Marketing Agreements
Joint marketing campaigns with partners require a co-marketing agreement
reviewed by Legal, separate from the vendor Data Processing Agreement
process, since co-marketing partners are not necessarily handling customer
data.

2.5 Embargo and Announcement Timing
Product announcements under embargo must not be shared internally beyond
the named launch team until 24 hours before public release, to reduce leak
risk; this internal embargo window is unrelated to the customer-facing
notification timelines Legal manages for data incidents.
