DATA PRIVACY AND RETENTION POLICY — LEGAL HANDBOOK, SECTION 5

5.1 Data Classification
Data is classified into three tiers: Public, Internal, and Restricted.
Restricted data includes customer PII, payment information, and health
records. Only Restricted-data-cleared employees (a designation separate
from general production access) may query tables containing this tier.

5.2 Retention Periods
Customer account data is retained for 7 years after account closure to
satisfy statutory recordkeeping requirements. Support ticket data is
retained for 2 years. Marketing engagement data (email opens, clicks) is
retained for 18 months, after which it is automatically purged unless the
customer has an active subscription.

5.3 Data Subject Access Requests (DSAR)
A verified data subject access request must be fulfilled within 30
calendar days under most applicable regulations, though some jurisdictions
require 45 days. Legal, not Engineering or Support, determines which
jurisdiction's timeline applies based on the customer's registered country.

5.4 Third-Party Data Sharing
No customer Restricted-tier data may be shared with a third-party vendor
without a signed Data Processing Agreement (DPA) reviewed by Legal. Marketing
and Analytics teams must use anonymized or aggregated data for any
third-party analytics tooling.

5.5 Breach Notification Coordination
This section cross-references the Security Incident Response process:
Legal owns the external, customer-facing and regulator-facing notification
timeline for any confirmed data exposure, while Security owns the internal
technical response and containment. The two timelines run in parallel, not
sequentially, once Security confirms exposure to Legal.
