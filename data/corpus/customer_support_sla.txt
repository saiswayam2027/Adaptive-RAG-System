CUSTOMER SUPPORT SLA POLICY — SUPPORT HANDBOOK, SECTION 2

2.1 Ticket Priority Levels
Priority-1 (P1): Customer's production system is fully down. First response
required within 15 minutes, 24/7. Priority-2 (P2): Major feature broken, no
workaround. First response within 2 hours during business hours.
Priority-3 (P3): Minor issue or question, first response within 1 business
day.

2.2 Escalation Path
A support ticket that goes 50% past its first-response SLA is automatically
escalated to the shift lead. If still unresolved at 100% past SLA, it
escalates to the Support Manager, and for P1 tickets specifically, also
pages the on-call Engineering team simultaneously rather than waiting for
sequential escalation.

2.3 Refund and Credit Authority
Support agents may issue service credits up to $100 without approval.
Credits between $100 and $1,000 require Team Lead approval. Anything above
$1,000, or any full refund request, requires Support Manager approval and
must be logged with a reason code for Finance's quarterly credit audit.

2.4 Customer Data Requests in Support Context
If a customer asks a support agent to delete their account data, the agent
must route this to the formal Data Subject Access Request process (see
Legal's Data Privacy Policy) rather than manually deleting records, since
manual deletion does not satisfy the audit trail requirements Legal needs
for regulatory compliance.

2.5 Outage Communication
During a P1 incident affecting multiple customers, Support posts to the
public status page within 30 minutes of confirmation and updates it at
least every 30 minutes until resolution, coordinating messaging with
Engineering's incident commander to avoid contradicting technical details.
