SECURITY INCIDENT RESPONSE — ENGINEERING HANDBOOK, SECTION 6

6.1 Severity Levels
Severity-1 (Sev-1): Active data breach, customer data exposed, or complete service
outage affecting all customers. Sev-1 requires immediate paging of the on-call
Security Lead and the VP of Engineering, regardless of time of day.

Severity-2 (Sev-2): Partial outage or a vulnerability with confirmed exploitation
attempts but no confirmed data loss. Sev-2 requires Security Lead notification
within 30 minutes.

Severity-3 (Sev-3): A discovered vulnerability with no evidence of exploitation.
Sev-3 issues are triaged during the next business day's security standup.

6.2 Error-Rate and Latency Thresholds
For canary evaluation, a release fails automatically if the p99 latency increases
by more than 15% over baseline, or if the error rate exceeds 1.5% during the
canary window. These thresholds are stricter than the 2% general rollback
threshold described in the Deployment Policy because canary traffic is a smaller,
more sensitive sample.

6.3 Post-Incident Reviews
Every Sev-1 and Sev-2 incident requires a written postmortem within 5 business
days. Postmortems must be blameless, must include a timeline reconstructed from
logs (not memory), and must be reviewed by the Security Lead before being shared
company-wide.

6.4 Data Breach Notification
If customer data is confirmed exposed, Legal and the Data Protection Officer must
be notified within 1 hour of confirmation, per compliance obligations. The
customer-facing notification timeline depends on jurisdiction and is determined
by Legal, not Engineering.
